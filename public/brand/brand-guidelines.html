Colour Usage Rules

  • Olive is the primary brand colour — use it for headers, CTAs, and key brand moments.
  • Cream is the default page background; never use pure white as a page background.
  • Black is reserved for body text and high-contrast UI elements only.
  • White is used for text on olive backgrounds and card surfaces.
  • Secondary colours support the primary palette — never use them as dominant colours.
  • Alert Red is functional only — never use it decoratively or as a brand colour.

05 — Photography Style
Visual language and imagery guidelines
Editorial Lifestyle
Real homes, real kitchens. Natural light, considered composition. Show stone in context — not as a product shot.
Texture Close-Ups
Macro shots highlighting the grain, movement, and depth of each surface. Warm, natural tones. No harsh studio lighting.
Architectural Spaces
Wide interior shots showcasing full benchtops in premium kitchen, bathroom, or commercial settings.
Human Connection
Subtle inclusion of people — a hand on stone, a meal prepared. Never forced. Always aspirational.

Colour & Tone Direction

Warmth
Lean warm — golden hour tones, soft yellows and creams. Avoid cool, blue-tinted photography.
Contrast
Medium contrast. Shadows should be soft and open, never harsh or dramatic.
Saturation
Slightly desaturated, natural. Avoid oversaturated colours or heavy post-processing filters.
Mood
Calm, considered, aspirational. The feeling of a quiet Sunday morning in a beautifully designed home.

What to Avoid

  • Harsh studio lighting or flash photography
  • Overly staged or artificial-looking scenes
  • Stock photography with generic models or settings
  • Cool, blue-tinted colour grading
  • Cluttered compositions without clear focal points
  • Low-resolution or pixelated imagery

06 — Voice & Tone
How Alpha Surfaces communicates

Brand Voice

Authoritative

We speak with confidence born from expertise. Our knowledge of surfaces, materials, and the Australian market is deep and genuine. We educate without lecturing.

Warm

We are approachable and human. Behind every surface is a family, a meal, a memory. We connect emotionally while maintaining professionalism.

Considered

Every word is intentional. We value clarity over cleverness, substance over style. Our communication reflects the same care we put into our surfaces.

Premium

Our language reflects the quality of our product. We avoid discount language, urgency tactics, or cheap marketing clichés. We let the product speak.

Writing Guidelines

Headlines

Use Concrette S. Keep headlines short and evocative. Lead with benefit or emotion. Avoid puns, jargon, or forced wordplay. Example: "Stone that tells a story." not "Rock-solid quality!"

Body Copy

Use Degular. Write in short, clear sentences. Aim for a reading level that is accessible but not simplistic. Use Australian English spelling (colour, not color).

CTAs

Be direct and specific. "Explore the Collection" not "Click Here". "Request a Sample" not "Learn More". Use sentence case, not all caps.

Product Names

Always capitalise collection and stone names. Use the full format: "Alpha Surfaces Calacatta Lucent" on first mention, then "Calacatta Lucent" subsequently. Never abbreviate to just "Calacatta".

07 — Digital Applications
Web, email, and social guidelines
Website
  • Primary domain: alphasurfaces.com.au
  • Default page background: Cream (#F3F1E6), never pure white
  • Navigation: Degular Semibold 11px uppercase, olive on cream
  • Hero sections: full-width imagery with Concrette S display text overlay
  • Buttons: olive background, white text, Degular Semibold 12px uppercase, 4px border-radius
  • Hover states: transition to Olive Mid (#7A7040) with 0.3s ease
  • Card components: white background, 1px border rgba(0,0,0,0.1), subtle shadow on hover
  • Responsive breakpoints: 1440px (desktop), 1024px (tablet), 768px (mobile landscape), 375px (mobile)
  • Image formats: WebP preferred, JPEG fallback. Lazy loading on below-the-fold images.
  • Favicon and touch icons must use the Alpha Surfaces brandmark on olive background
Email
  • Email width: 600px maximum
  • Header: olive background with white brandmark logo, centered
  • Body background: Cream (#F3F1E6)
  • Body font: system sans-serif stack (Arial, Helvetica) as fallback for Degular
  • CTA buttons: olive background, white text, minimum 44px tap target height
  • Footer: dark olive background with white text, unsubscribe link, physical address
  • Always include plain-text version alongside HTML
Social Media
  • Profile images: brandmark on olive background, square crop
  • Cover images: editorial lifestyle photography with subtle olive overlay
  • Post imagery: consistent warm tone, on-brand photography only
  • Text overlays on images: Concrette S Medium, white with drop shadow for legibility
  • Hashtags: #AlphaSurfaces #StoneStory #ZeroSilica #AustralianMade
  • Tone on social: slightly more casual but still considered. Never use slang or excessive emoji.
  • Preferred platforms: Instagram, LinkedIn, Pinterest. Consistent posting schedule.

09 — Do's & Don'ts
Common mistakes to avoid
✓ DO
✓ Use the brand colours exactly as specified — no approximations
✓ Always use Concrette S for headings and Degular for body text
✓ Maintain clear space around the logo at all times
✓ Use warm, natural photography that reflects the brand mood
✓ Write in Australian English (colour, centre, specialise)
✓ Capitalise collection and stone names consistently
✓ Use cream (#F3F1E6) as the default background, not white
✓ Get approval before creating any co-branded materials
✓ Use high-resolution imagery (minimum 300dpi for print)
✓ Reference these guidelines for every new brand touchpoint
✗ DON'T
✗ Alter the brand colours or use similar-but-different shades
✗ Substitute other fonts for Concrette S or Degular
✗ Stretch, rotate, or add effects to the logo
✗ Use cold, blue-tinted, or heavily filtered photography
✗ Use American English spellings (color, center, specialize)
✗ Abbreviate or informally shorten stone or collection names
✗ Use pure white (#FFFFFF) as a page or section background
✗ Create partner materials without brand team approval
✗ Use low-resolution, pixelated, or stock photography
✗ Use discount language, urgency tactics, or cheap marketing clichés
